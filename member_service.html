FAQs

Do you know?

  • Cornea is the clear tissue covering the front of the eye.
  • If the cornea becomes cloudy from disease, injury, infection, or poor nutrition, vision is dramatically reduced or lost.
  • Corneal blindness can be treated by replacing the damaged cornea with a healthy donated human cornea, which can only be obtained through eye donation.
  • One eye donation can give sight to two corneal-blind individuals.

What is Eye Donation?

  • Eye donation means donating the eyes of a person after death for transplantation with family consent.

How & When to Donate?

  • Eyes should be donated within 6-8 hours of death.
  • Anyone can be a donor, regardless of age, sex, blood group, or religion.
  • People with cataracts or who wear spectacles can donate eyes.
  • Individuals with hypertension or diabetes can also donate eyes.

Why Donate Eyes?

  • Blindness causes social and economic burdens on individuals, communities, and nations.
  • There is a significant shortage of donated eyes in our country.
  • The eyes must be removed within 6 hours of death. Please inform the nearest eye bank immediately.
  • The eye bank team will conduct a simple, bloodless procedure within 15-20 minutes.

Instructions for Donating Eyes

  • Fill out the pledge form and send it to the nearest eye bank to register as an eye donor and receive a donor card.
  • Spread awareness about eye donation; every word counts in promoting this cause.
  • When donating the eye of the deceased, call the nearest eye bank (1919) within 6 hours of death.
